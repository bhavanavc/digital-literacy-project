Social Media Do’s and Don’ts Checklist

Do’s:
- Think before you post
- Use respectful and professional language
- Verify information before sharing
- Protect your personal information
- Maintain a positive digital footprint

Don’ts:
- Do not share fake news or unverified content
- Avoid posting offensive or harmful comments
- Do not overshare personal details
- Avoid cyberbullying or trolling
- Do not misuse someone else’s content without credit


Part C – 100–150 Word Report

Poor digital communication can lead to serious misunderstandings. For example, a student once sent a message to a professor saying, “Send me the notes ASAP,” without any greeting or polite tone. The professor found the message rude and unprofessional, which negatively affected their impression of the student. As a result, the student did not receive timely help.

This situation could have been handled better by using proper email etiquette. The student should have included a polite greeting, a clear request, and respectful language such as “Could you please share the notes when convenient?” Maintaining professionalism in digital communication helps build positive relationships, avoids conflicts, and ensures that messages are received in the intended manner.
